Athletics

Man has no limit

About athletics

Athletics is often regarded as the foundation of competitive sports because it showcases the fundamental human abilities of running, jumping, and throwing.
It consists of a wide variety of events, including sprints, middle-distance races, marathons, long jump, high jump, javelin, and shot put.

For centuries, athletics has played an important role in sporting competitions, particularly in the Olympic Games, where athletes from around the world compete at the highest level. Success in athletics requires dedication, discipline, and consistent training.

Athletics celebrates individual achievement while encouraging athletes to continually challenge their physical and mental limits.
The sport has produced some of the world's most recognized athletes and continues to inspire future generations to pursue excellence. Whether competing professionally or participating recreationally, athletics promotes healthy living, determination, and self-improvement.

History

Athletics is one of the oldest forms of sport, with its origins dating back to the ancient Olympic Games in Greece around 776 BC.
Early competitions focused on running events, which later expanded to include jumping and throwing disciplines.

Over the centuries, athletics evolved into a structured sport with standardized rules and international competitions.
Today, it is a major part of events such as the Olympic Games and World Athletics Championships. Athletics continues to inspire athletes worldwide by showcasing speed, strength, endurance, and determination.

Events

Running Events

Running events in athletics include sprints, middle-distance, long-distance, hurdles, relays, marathons, and cross-country races, each testing speed, endurance, and strategy

Jumping Events

Jumping events in athletics include the long jump, high jump, pole vault, and triple jump, each testing explosive power and technique.

Throwing Events

Throwing events in athletics include the shot put, discus throw, javelin throw, and hammer throw, each testing strength and precision.

Records

Athletics has seen numerous world records set by exceptional athletes over the years.
These records represent the pinnacle of human performance:

  • Usain Bolt holds the world record for the 100m and 200m sprints.
  • Florence Griffith-Joyner holds the world record for the 100m and 200m sprints for women
  • Javier Sotomayor holds the world record for the high jump.
  • Mike Powell holds the world record for the long jump.

Fun facts

Here are some interesting facts about athletics:

  • The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens, Greece in 1896.
  • Athletics is the only sport that has been part of every modern Olympic Games since 1896.
  • The 100-meter dash is often called the "most prestigious race in the world."
  • The shot put is the only event in athletics where men and women compete against each other.
