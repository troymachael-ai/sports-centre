About Basketball

Basketball is one of the fastest-growing sports in the world and is known for its speed, skill, and excitement.
Played between two teams of five players, the objective is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's basket while preventing them from doing the same.

The sport was invented in 1891 and has since evolved into a global spectacle. Professional leagues such as the NBA have helped basketball gain immense popularity, producing iconic athletes and memorable moments that have inspired millions of fans worldwide. Basketball requires a combination of athleticism, teamwork, quick decision-making, and technical ability.
Players must constantly adapt to changing situations during a game, making it one of the most dynamic sports to watch and play. Beyond competition, basketball promotes fitness, cooperation, and personal development. It remains a favorite sport among young people because of its accessibility and exciting style of play.

History

Basketball was formed in 1891 by by James Naismith at the International YMCA Training School in Springfield, Massachusetts, using peach baskets as goals.
Naismith published 13 original rules, emphasizing passing, non-contact play, and stationary ball handling.
The first public game occurred on March 11, 1892, in Springfield, Massachusetts.

Basketball spread rapidly through YMCA networks to Canada, France, England, Australia, China, India, and Japan by the early 1900s The 5-a-side rule was established in 1897
The first professional league formed in 1898, though early pro basketball grew slowly making its olympic debut in 1936 After World War II, the sport expanded internationally, especially in Europe and Asia with the NBA being formed in 1949 Today Basketball is one of the world's most popular sports, played globally and governed internationally by FIBA

Basketball Techniques

Dribbling

Dribbling is the act of bouncing the ball continuously with one hand while moving around the court. It allows players to advance the ball, evade defenders, and create scoring opportunities.

Shooting

Shooting is the act of attempting to score points by throwing the ball into the basket.

Passing

Passing is the skill of transferring the ball to a teammate effectively.

Defense

Defense is the set of techniques used to prevent the opposing team from scoring, including blocking and stealing.
